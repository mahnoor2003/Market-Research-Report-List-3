<p><p>Bioceramics are classified into different types based on their composition and properties. Alumina-type bioceramics are known for their high strength and wear resistance, making them suitable for orthopedic implants. Zirconia-type bioceramics are biocompatible and have excellent mechanical properties, making them ideal for dental applications. Other types of bioceramics include calcium phosphate-based materials and bioactive glasses, which are used for bone regeneration and tissue engineering. Each type of bioceramic offers unique benefits and applications in the medical field.</p></p>
