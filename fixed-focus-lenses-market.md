<p><p>Fixed-focus lenses are categorized into three main types in the market: Fixed Iris, Manual Iris, and Auto Iris. Fixed Iris lenses have a fixed aperture, which means the amount of light entering the lens cannot be adjusted. Manual Iris lenses allow the user to manually adjust the aperture based on lighting conditions. Auto Iris lenses have a built-in mechanism to automatically adjust the aperture based on the lighting environment. Each type of lens caters to different needs and preferences in terms of control over the aperture.</p></p>
