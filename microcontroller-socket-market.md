<p><p>Microcontroller sockets come in various types such as Dual Inline Package (DIP), Ball Grid Array (BGA), Quad Flat Package (QFP), Small Outline Package (SOP), and Small Outline Integrated Circuit (SOIC). Each type has a different design and structure, catering to specific needs and requirements in the market. DIP sockets are commonly used for through-hole mounting, while BGA sockets are ideal for compact designs. QFP, SOP, and SOIC sockets offer different advantages in terms of size, ease of assembly, and performance, making them popular choices in the microcontroller market.</p></p>
