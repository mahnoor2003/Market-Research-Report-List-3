<p><p>Nitrogenous water soluble fertilizers can be classified into three main types: Macroelement WSF, Microelement WSF, and Others. Macroelement WSF contains major nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, which are crucial for plant growth. Microelement WSF includes trace elements like iron, zinc, and manganese that are required in small quantities for plant development. The category of Others encompasses specialized water soluble fertilizers with unique formulations to cater to specific plant needs or growing conditions.</p></p>

<p><p>Nitrogenous water-soluble fertilizers are used in various applications such as foliar application, soilless culture, soaking seeds and dipping roots, sprinkling irrigation, and others. Foliar application involves spraying the fertilizer directly onto the leaves for quick absorption. Soilless culture refers to growing plants in a medium other than soil, with the fertilizer being added to the irrigation system. Soaking seeds and dipping roots involves treating seeds or plant roots with the fertilizer solution before planting. Sprinkling irrigation involves applying the fertilizer through an irrigation system.</p></p>
