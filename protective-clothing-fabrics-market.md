<p><p>Protective clothing fabrics can be divided into two main types: inherent fabric and treated fabric. </p><p>Inherent fabric is made with fibers that have inherent flame-resistant properties, making them durable and long-lasting in terms of protection against extreme heat and flames. </p><p>On the other hand, treated fabric is manufactured with fibers that are treated with special chemicals to enhance their flame-resistant properties.</p><p>Both types of fabrics are widely used in industries where workers are at risk of exposure to heat, flames, or hazardous materials.</p></p>
