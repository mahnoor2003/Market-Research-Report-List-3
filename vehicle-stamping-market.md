<p><p>Vehicle stamping is a process used in automobile manufacturing to shape metal sheets into components like doors, hoods, and panels. Hot stamping involves heating the metal sheets and then quickly cooling them to increase their strength. Cold stamping, on the other hand, shapes metal sheets at room temperature without heating. Each method has its advantages and is chosen based on the specific requirements of the component being produced. Both hot and cold stamping play a crucial role in the automotive industry in creating high-quality and durable vehicle parts.</p></p>
