<p><p>Viscosifiers in the oil and gas industry are used to increase the viscosity of drilling fluids for better hole cleaning and suspension of cuttings. There are two main types of viscosifiers: organic viscosifiers, which are derived from natural sources like plant or animal products, and inorganic viscosifiers, which are synthetic chemicals. Organic viscosifiers are environmentally friendly and biodegradable, while inorganic viscosifiers offer higher stability and performance in extreme conditions. Both types play a crucial role in optimizing drilling operations.</p></p>
