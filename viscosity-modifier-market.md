<p><p>Viscosity modifiers are classified into different types based on their source and composition. Minerals, such as clay or silica, are commonly used as viscosity modifiers to thicken fluids. Hydrocolloids, like guar gum or xanthan gum, are natural polymers that also enhance viscosity. Synthetic polymers, such as polyacrylates or polyethylene glycol, are widely used to modify viscosity in various industries. Other types of viscosity modifiers may include proteins, cellulose derivatives, or even certain chemicals specifically designed to alter the flow characteristics of a fluid.</p></p>
